Our Story

Ashley

You know the line, "We found love in a hopeless place?" That was our story. On March 11, 2023, we both spent the day out and happened to find our way into a sports bar attached to a hostel in Barcelona. I ran into a friend of a friend, he invited me to his table, and that was the first time we met. I was there with my friend Dan and we all spent the evening hanging out together. At one point, I whispered to Dan that I thought Eoin was cute. When I went to the bar, Eoin asked Dan about us being together, and Dan replied, "Oh no… she's into you."

From that moment, everything shifted. Friends left one by one, and when it was just the two of us, he kissed me. Everything felt so effortless and just made sense. We dated, and I knew quickly he was nothing like anyone I'd dated before — we often joke that we share a brain because we are always thinking the same things. Soon after, I asked him to be my boyfriend, since he was taking so long to ask me.

That August I went to Ireland with him for the first time and met his entire family. Everything just felt right. They were so lovely and welcoming and were the type of family I dreamt of marrying into.

Ashley

Eoin

I almost didn't go out that night. I'd been golfing all day, I was tired, and Barcelona had worn me down. But something told me to go. I ended up in this random sports bar attached to a hostel, and there she was. My friend introduced us, and from the very first conversation I knew there was something different about her. We talked for hours. The bar emptied out around us and neither of us noticed.

When it was just us left, I kissed her — and that was it. Everything after that felt inevitable, like we were always meant to find each other in that ridiculous little bar. Over the next few weeks we were inseparable. She asked me to be her boyfriend before I could ask her, which is the most Ashley thing imaginable. I knew straight away this was different from anything I'd had before.

When she came to Ireland and met my family, I watched her fit right in — laughing with my mam, charming my dad, winning over everyone. I stood there thinking: this is the one. And then in September her dad and stepmom met us in Madrid, and it felt like our two worlds were finally coming together. I knew then that this was it — the start of everything.

Eoin

The Venue

Ballintubbert
Gardens & House

The heart of our story unfolds at the stunning Ballintubbert Gardens & House, a historic Georgian estate set on 14 acres of enchanting gardens and sweeping grounds. Once home to celebrated figures such as poet Cecil Day-Lewis and actors Sebastian Shaw and Sir John Thrift, the estate sits on land sacred since Druidic times with a mineral-rich well where people have journeyed for its healing waters for generations.

Here, at the Fountain Gardens, we will exchange our vows, hopefully under the warm Irish sun, before an evening filled with love, music, and celebration with all of you.

Location

Ballintubbert House, Ballintubbert, Stradbally, Co. Laois, R14 E954, Ireland

How to Get There

Ballintubbert Gardens & House is nestled in the countryside of County Laois, approximately one hour from Dublin by car. It’s easily accessible for guests coming from the city or surrounding areas.

From Dublin:

  • Take the M7 motorway heading southwest.
  • Exit at Junction 16 for the R445 toward Portlaoise / Stradbally.
  • Follow local signage to Ballintubbert Gardens & House.

For guests wishing to make a weekend of it, there are a range of lovely accommodation options nearby. Alternatively, guests are very welcome to stay in Dublin and enjoy a scenic drive down for the celebration. We recommend arranging car sharing or taxis for convenience and to ensure everyone can fully enjoy the day. Please don’t drink and drive.

Where to Stay

Accommodation options depend on how you’d like to spend the weekend. You can stay in Dublin or choose accommodation in nearby towns and villages. Please don’t drink and drive—the legal limit is lower than in the United States. Cabs are available!
