Our Story

Ashley

You know the line, “we found love in a hopeless place?” That was us. On March 11, 2023, after a calçotada lunch (me) and golf (him), we both ended up at a random hostel sports bar in Barcelona (a place I would not typically frequent 🙃).

A mutual friend pulled me to his table, where I met Eoin. We spent the night talking and laughing, and I told my friend that I thought he was cute. At one point, he asked my friend if we were together. He replied, “Oh no… I have a boyfriend, and she’s into you.” After that, everything shifted, and by the end of the night, he kissed me.

From the start, everything felt easy. Within weeks, I knew he was different—we often joke we share one brain because we’re always thinking the same things. On May 14, I asked him to be my boyfriend. That year, we met each other’s families. The following year, we moved in together, and on our first anniversary, we adopted our cat, Roísín.

On May 27, 2025, at Muckross House, in front of our parents, Eoin asked me to marry him. It was an easy yes and I wouldn’t have changed a thing (okay, maybe I would have changed my outfit).

Ashley

Eoin

When I think about mine and Ashley's story, the first thing that comes to mind is how fast it's all gone. The last few years have flown, and now we're in a place I used to dream about when we first met.

It started in a bar. I'd been golfing and wasn't planning on drinking, but got talked into it. We ended up in Belushis, and while we were there, Ashley and our friend Dan came over. I assumed they were together, so I was confused when it felt like Ashley was flirting, but even in a group I felt drawn to her. We clicked instantly. After learning Dan was just her friend, and after a couple of espresso martinis, everyone else left and I leaned in for a kiss. We spent the rest of the night hopping around Barcelona.

The next day, I couldn't stop thinking about her. I took the risk and asked her to hang out. We spent the day in the park, completely at ease, talking about everything and realising how aligned we were.

From then, I just knew. Being with Ashley felt easy, safe, and right, and it always has.

Eoin

Gardens & House

The heart of our story unfolds at the stunning Ballintubbert Gardens & House, a historic Georgian estate set on 14 acres of enchanting gardens and sweeping grounds. Once home to celebrated figures such as poet Cecil Day-Lewis and actors Sebastian Shaw and Sir John Hurt, the estate sits on land sacred since Druidic times with a mineral-rich well where people have journeyed for its healing waters for generations.

At the Fountain Gardens, we will exchange our vows, hopefully under the warm Irish sun, before an evening filled with love, music, and celebration with all of you.

How to Get There

Ballintubbert Gardens & House is nestled in the countryside of County Laois, approximately one hour from Dublin by car or train. It’s easily accessible for guests coming from the city or surrounding areas.

From Dublin via Car:

  • Take the M7 motorway heading southwest
  • Exit at Junction 16 for the R445 toward Portlaoise / Stradbally
  • Follow local signage to Ballintubbert Gardens & House

From Dublin via Train:

  • Take the Irish Rail from Dublin Heuston Central Train Station → Portlaoise Train Station

For guests wishing to make a weekend of it, there are a range of lovely accommodation options nearby. Alternatively, guests are very welcome to stay in Dublin and enjoy a scenic drive down for the celebration. We recommend arranging car sharing or taxis for convenience and to ensure everyone can fully enjoy the day. Please don’t drink and drive.

Where to Stay

Things to Do Nearby

Whether you’re arriving a few days early or extending your trip, County Laois and the surrounding midlands have plenty to offer. Here are some of our favourite spots near the venue.

Ballyadams Castle (5 min) — A historic 14th-century castle ruin surrounded by scenic countryside, perfect for a short visit or photos.

Abbeyleix Heritage House (Abbeyleix, 20 min) — Local heritage museum with exhibits on community history.

Aghaboe Abbey (Abbeyleix, 25 min) — Historic abbey ruins with atmospheric charm.

Sham Castle & Gothic Ruins (near Abbeyleix, 30 min) — Historic ruins set in woodland, offering a quiet spot to explore and enjoy the scenery.

White Castle (Athy, 30 min) — A historic castle/tower house in the centre of Athy, originally built in the early 15th century.

Ballaghmore Castle (Ballaghmore, 25 min) — Small medieval castle in the village of Ballaghmore.

Cullahill Castle (Cullahill, 35 min) — Ruined castles set in scenic countryside for a quieter exploration.

Rock of Dunamase (Portlaoise, 20 min) — Spectacular hilltop medieval fortress with breathtaking views over the midlands — perfect for photos and a short adventure.

Emo Court House (Emo, 25 min) — Stunning neo-classical estate with gardens and walking trails to explore.

Morett Castle (Emo, 20 min) — Historic 16th-century castle ruins with atmospheric views and medieval intrigue.

Donaghmore Workhouse & Agricultural Museum (Donaghmore, 20 min) — Fascinating insight into Irish social history.

Timahoe Heritage Centre & Round Tower (Timahoe, 20 min) — A 12th-century round tower and small museum in the quaint village of Timahoe.

Abbeyleix Bog Project (Abbeyleix, 25 min) — A beautiful community-run peatland nature reserve with boardwalk trails, wildlife spotting, and peaceful walks through restored bogland.

Glenbarrow Car Park (Slieve Bloom area, 35 min) — Gateway to walks in Glenbarrow Woods and trails in the Slieve Bloom foothills — great for longer hikes or scenic picnics.

Cathole Falls — Pretty waterfall setting ideal for nature lovers and photographers.

Togher Woods — Peaceful woodland walk perfect for a morning stroll.

Heywood Gardens (Ballinakill, 30 min) — Beautiful Lutyens-inspired gardens and woodland walks.

Stradbally Woodland Railway (Stradbally, 5–10 min) — Scenic steam/tourist railway near Stradbally town. Ireland’s first volunteer heritage railway, operating since 1969.

Lisduff Adventure Farm (near Durrow, 30 min) — Family-friendly farm with animals and outdoor play. Seasonal.

Maize Maze — Dealgrove Farm (Abbeyleix, 20 min) — Seasonal maize maze and farm fun (check opening times).

Ballykilcavan Brewery (near Portlaoise, 30 min) — Award-winning craft brewery with tours and tastings — ideal for adults who appreciate a quality pint.

Kildare Village: Luxury Shopping Outlets (Kildare, 25 min) — A popular open-air shopping destination with over 100 boutiques offering designer and lifestyle brands, cafes and restaurants.

  • Gardens & Trails: Laois is part of the Laois Gardens Trail, with beautiful gardens like Heywood Gardens just a short drive away.
  • Historical Walks: There are a number of ancient sites and ruins throughout the county that are great for a self-guided heritage day.
  • Outdoor Adventures: The Slieve Bloom Mountains are a bit further out but excellent for hiking and scenic drives, if you want a longer day trip.
